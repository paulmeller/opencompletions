# Risk Scoring Rubric

Use the following criteria to assign a risk rating to each clause in a contract.

## High Risk

A clause is **High Risk** if it meets any of the following criteria:

- Creates significant legal or financial exposure for one party
- Imposes one-sided obligations with no reciprocal protections
- Contains terms that may be unenforceable or unconscionable
- Assigns ownership of pre-existing intellectual property without compensation
- Includes unlimited liability or indemnification covering the other party's negligence
- Contains non-compete terms that are overbroad in scope, duration, or geography
- Allows unilateral termination without reasonable notice or cure periods
- Lacks specificity on governing law or dispute resolution

## Medium Risk

A clause is **Medium Risk** if it meets any of the following criteria:

- Contains unusual terms that could cause issues under certain circumstances
- Uses ambiguous language that could be interpreted unfavorably
- Includes auto-renewal provisions without adequate notice requirements
- Defines vague performance standards without measurable metrics
- Creates moderate imbalance in rights or obligations between parties

## Low Risk

A clause is **Low Risk** if it meets any of the following criteria:

- Contains minor concerns that are unlikely to cause significant issues
- Uses standard but slightly unfavorable terms common in the industry
- Includes terms that are generally enforceable and balanced
- Poses minimal legal or financial exposure
